Here is a draft 5 page children's book about Mattapus the platypus who wants to be called by his full name:

Page 1 
Once there was a platypus named Mattapus. All of his friends called him Matt for short. But Mattapus didn't like this one bit! "My name is Mattapus!" he would say. But his friends just laughed and continued to call him Matt.

Page 2
One day, Mattapus decided he had enough. When his best friend Nutty the squirrel called out "Hey Matt! Want to go swimming?" Mattapus stomped his foot and said "No! My name is not Matt, it's Mattapus!" 

Nutty was confused. "But Matt, I've called you that since we were kids. Why do you want me to call you your full name all of a sudden?"

"Because Nutty," said Mattapus, "Mattapus is my name and I want to be called by my real name!"

Page 3 
So Mattapus gathered all his friends—Nutty the squirrel, Bill the beaver, Sally the salamander, and more. "Attention friends!" he announced. "From now on, you must call me by full name, Mattapus!"

His friends glanced at each other in confusion. "But...we've always called you Matt?" said Sally.

"Not anymore!" declared Mattapus. "To show you how serious I am, I will not respond anymore if you call me Matt."

Sure enough, when his friends tried to get his attention by calling "Hey Matt!" Mattapus just ignored them.

Page 4
As the days went by, Mattapus' friends realized he was serious. No matter how many times they shouted "Matt!" he never responded or even looked their way. Finally Bill the beaver approached him cautiously and said "Um, excuse me, Mattapus? Would you like to have lunch?"

Mattapus turned with a big smile. "Why yes, I'd love to Bill!" he said happily. All his other friends looked at each other with surprise. 

Page 5
From then on, all of Mattapus' friends remembered to call him by his full name. It took some getting used to at first, but soon shouting "Hey Mattapus!" came naturally to them. 

Mattapus was proud to hear his full platypus name said with respect. And so, Mattapus the platypus lived happily ever after in his forest home, surrounded by friends who always remembered exactly what to call him.

The end!
