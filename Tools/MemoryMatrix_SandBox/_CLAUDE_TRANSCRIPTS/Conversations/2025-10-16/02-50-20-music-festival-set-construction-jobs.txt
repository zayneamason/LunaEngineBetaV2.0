## **🎓 GETTING STARTED - NO EXPERIENCE PATH**

### **If You Have ZERO Experience:**

**Option 1: Entry-Level/Helper Positions**
- Start as an entry-level carpenter helper - learn on the job with high school diploma or GED
- Look for "carpenter helper," "carpenter assistant," "general laborer" positions
- Entry level starts around $21-35/hour in LA area

**Option 2: Trade School (Fastest Path - 6-9 months)**
- California carpentry certificate programs can be completed in as little as 6-9 months, costing $750-$1,000 for entry level
- Search: "carpentry trade school Los Angeles"
- Recommended: College of the Canyons, LA Trade Tech

**Option 3: Apprenticeship (Traditional - 4 years)**
- California carpentry apprenticeships are about 4 years requiring 4,800 work hours and 612 class hours
- Search apprenticeships: California Department of Industrial Relations website

### **Skills to Develop:**
- Power tools (table saws, chop saws, routers), reading blueprints, theatrical carpentry basics, basic rigging knowledge
- OSHA 10 or 30 certification (highly desirable)
- Own basic hand tools (hammer, tape measure, level, etc.)

## **💡 INSIDER TIPS FOR SUCCESS**

### **How to Stand Out (No Experience):**

1. **Show Up Early, Stay Late**
   - First one there = noticed
   - Last to leave = remembered

2. **Say "Yes" to Everything Initially**
   - Night shifts? Yes.
   - Weekend setup? Yes.
   - Last-minute call? Yes.
   - (Builds reputation fast)

3. **Ask Questions the Right Way**
   - Not: "What do I do?"
   - Yes: "Should I use 2" or 3" screws for this?"
   - (Shows you're thinking)

4. **Watch and Learn**
   - Shadow experienced carpenters
   - Notice techniques
   - Ask "why" they do things certain ways

5. **Take Initiative**
   - Clean up without being asked
   - Organize tools
   - Prep next task
   - (Shows professionalism)

6. **Bring Your Own Tools**
   - Shows commitment
   - Won't have to wait to borrow
   - Take care of them = respected

7. **Document Your Work**
   - Take photos (ask permission first)
   - Build portfolio
   - Show progression

8. **Network Aggressively**
   - Get numbers from crew members
   - Stay in touch
   - Most jobs come from referrals

## **🚨 COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID**

1. **Waiting for "Perfect" Job**
   - Take general laborer role if needed
   - Experience > perfect title

2. **Not Getting OSHA Certified**
   - This alone eliminates you from 50% of jobs
   - Takes one day, costs $50

3. **Underestimating Physical Demands**
   - Train/workout if not in shape
   - Injuries = can't work

4. **Poor Communication**
   - Check emails daily
   - Return calls promptly
   - If you can't make a call, say so ASAP

5. **Burning Bridges**
   - Small industry
   - Today's helper is tomorrow's hiring manager
   - Be professional with EVERYONE

6. **Not Following Up**
   - Most people apply once and forget
   - Follow up = shows interest

7. **Lying About Experience**
   - Will be exposed on day one
   - Better to be honest, eager to learn
