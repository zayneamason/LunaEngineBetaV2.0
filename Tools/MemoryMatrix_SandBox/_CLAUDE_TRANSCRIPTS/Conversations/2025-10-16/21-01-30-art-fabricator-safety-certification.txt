 Getting OSHA certified is a smart move for an art fabricator! Here's what you need to know:

**Main OSHA Training Options:**

**OSHA 10-Hour General Industry** - This is probably your best starting point. It covers basic safety topics like hazard communication, PPE, electrical safety, and materials handling. You'll get an official OSHA card upon completion.

**OSHA 30-Hour General Industry** - More comprehensive training that goes deeper into safety topics. This is better if you're in a supervisory role or want more thorough knowledge.

**Where to Get Certified:**

You can take these courses through:
- OSHA-authorized training providers (both online and in-person)
- Local community colleges or technical schools
- Union training centers
- Private safety training companies

The courses typically cost $60-150 for the 10-hour and $150-300 for the 30-hour, depending on the provider.

**Specific to Art Fabrication:**

Given your work, you might also want training in:
- Respiratory protection (if you work with resins, paints, or do welding)
- Lockout/tagout procedures
- Confined spaces (if relevant)
- Hazard communication/GHS
