1. National Insurance Crime Bureau (NICB): 
   - They offer a free VINCheck service on their website.
   - You can enter a Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) to see if it's been reported stolen.

2. Local Police Department:
   - Many police departments can run a stolen vehicle check if you provide the VIN or license plate number.

3. State DMV (Department of Motor Vehicles):
   - Some state DMVs offer services to check if a vehicle has been reported stolen.

4. Online Services:
   - There are paid services like Carfax or AutoCheck that include stolen vehicle information in their vehicle history reports.

5. National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA):
   - They have a free VIN lookup tool that can provide some vehicle information, including recall notices.

It's important to note that access to comprehensive stolen vehicle databases is often restricted to law enforcement agencies for security reasons. If you suspect a vehicle is stolen, it's best to contact your local police department directly.

Remember, these methods typically require the vehicle's VIN or license plate number. Always be cautious when purchasing used vehicles and consider getting a full vehicle history report.
