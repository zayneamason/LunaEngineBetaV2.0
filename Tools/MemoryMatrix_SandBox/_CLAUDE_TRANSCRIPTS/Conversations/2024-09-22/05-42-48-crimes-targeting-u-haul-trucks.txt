what is a bait and switch

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ASSISTANT] 2024-09-22T06:05:24.627935Z
 A bait and switch is a deceptive sales tactic where a business advertises or offers an attractive deal (the "bait") to lure in customers, but then attempts to sell them a different, often more expensive product or service (the "switch") once they arrive or engage with the business. This practice is generally considered unethical and is illegal in many jurisdictions.

In the context of these U-Haul reviews, potential bait and switch tactics could include:

1. Reservations not honored: Multiple reviewers mentioned making reservations for trucks, only to arrive and be told no vehicles were available. This could be seen as baiting customers with the promise of a vehicle, then switching to a situation where they either have to accept a different (possibly more expensive) option or go elsewhere.

2. Vehicle condition: Some customers reported being given dirty or damaged vehicles, which is different from the expectation set when renting from a reputable company.

3. Pricing discrepancies: Several reviewers mentioned being charged more than expected or for services they didn't use, which could be seen as a form of bait and switch if the initial quoted price was significantly lower.

4. Forced upgrades: While not explicitly mentioned in these reviews, a common bait and switch tactic in the rental industry is to offer a low price on a basic model, then claim it's unavailable and try to upsell to a more expensive option.

5. Reservation recreation: One reviewer specifically mentioned being told they had to create a new reservation on-site, potentially at a different rate, despite having already made one online.

It's important to note that while these practices are concerning, true bait and switch tactics require intent to deceive. Some of these issues could be due to poor management or genuine mistakes. However, the frequency of these complaints suggests a pattern that at least warrants further investigation.

If you encounter what you believe to be bait and switch tactics, it's advisable to document everything, refuse any charges you didn't agree to, and report the incident to the company's corporate office and local consumer protection agencies if necessary.
