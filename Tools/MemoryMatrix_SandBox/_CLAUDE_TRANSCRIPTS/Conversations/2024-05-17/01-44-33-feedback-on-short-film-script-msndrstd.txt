**_MSNDRSTD_**

**A Short Film By: Jonathan T. Boyle**

**_About the Film_**

MSNDRSTD is a short film inspired by the motivational self-help memoir,

“The Misunderstood Millennial” written by 4x Author Jonathan T. Boyle. This powerful short film is a dramatical picture inspired by a true story. This story shows the path of a former athlete who’s original dream was to pursue professional basketball but his life crumbles after the death of his closest best friend due to sudden diagnosed terminal cancer. While grieving from a death of a considered loved one, navigating college life, peer pressure, identity struggles, and mental health battles, this compelling film goes from tragedy to triumph. This film inspires you to never trade your vision for approval and keep the faith while enduring on your life marathon.

**_Film Locations_**

1\. Inglewood Cemetery

2\. Venice Beach (Dispensary & Blacktop)

3\. USC Campus

4\. Therapy Session

5\. The Church 

**_Scene Location Themes_**

Short Film Duration: 5-10 Minutes

**_Film Script_**

_First Location: Inglewood Cemetery_

_Fade In_

**_Action: Jay and Frank walking to the gravesite to mourn and places roses after leaving the car_**

_Dialogue:_

JAY: So, what do we do now?...

FRANK: We adjust.

JAY: It’s hard to adjust when you’re lost and in pain.

Frank: Well, what do you normally do when you feel lost?

Jay: The only thing I ever did was just hoop. So now what?

Frank: I have the perfect place to get your mind off things

**_Action: Walked back to the car to go to the next destination_**

Jay: So, where we headed now bro?

Frank: My favorite playground, Venice Beach

Fade out to next scene.

_Second Location: Venice Beach_

**Action: Jay and Frank walking by the infamous “Venice” sign towards the beach.**

Dialogue:

JAY: So, this is your favorite playground huh?

FRANK: Yup… nothing like Venice. This is where I always come to get my head right. The best beach in LA

JAY: What brought you out to the West Coast?

FRANK: I’m from the Motherland… grew up back in the Midwest too, but always felt drawn to tell stories here. So, I came to Hollywood to pursue acting and my creative dreams.

**_Action: Jay & Frank conversating and walking to the basketball courts. Frank picks_ up a ball and then tosses it to Jay.**

FRANK: You still got it right?

JAY: … Something like that… but I think that dream is over now. It’s time to find a new one. (Shooting at the free-throw line)

FRANK: I know it’s hard to maneuver through grief, and pain especially as black men. Keep dreaming through the pain and ask God to lead you.. You ever thought of going to therapy. I think it will help you unpack things.

**_Action: Jay puts the ball down and walks away._**

Dialogue:

FRANK: Ay bro… where u going?

JAY: Man forget therapy… I got a better idea.

**_Action: Jay walks to the dispensary and Frank follows him in._**

JAY: This is my therapy… talks with the clouds…

BUDTENDER: Welcome in, what can I help you find today?

Jay: I’m looking for something uplifting… maybe a Sativa you recommend?

BUDTENDER: We have a special right now for first timers.. I got this gelato for you.

JAY: That works .. thanks for the help!

Action: Jay gives the budtender the cash while Frank watches and then they walk out.

Frank: Ay let me that bro.. I’m stressed too.

JAY: I feel you, it’s hard out here in LA at times. But this helps me cope and get through it.

Action:: Frank and Jay smoking and leaving the Venice area

FRANK: You hungry? I got the munchies… let’s get some food around here

JAY: Man, I’m always down for food, that’s a bet.

Action: They walk into a local restaurant and get in line behind a man looking at the menu

JAY: Excuse me sir, this is our first time here… what do you usually order?

Action: Man in line turns around to shake their hand and greet them.

MARK: What’s good… I’m Mark, nice to meet ya’ll. Where ya’ll from? This place is bomb.. Get their wings

[ASSISTANT] 2024-05-17T01:44:43.670823Z
 Here are a few thoughts on the script:

Positives:
- The premise of following a character dealing with grief, mental health struggles, and finding purpose is an emotionally resonant one that could make for a compelling short film.
- The different locations provide visual variety and opportunities to explore different environments/tones.
- The naturalistic dialogue between the two friends has an authentic, conversational feel to it.
- Touching on issues like therapy, substance use for coping, and the challenges of being a Black man adds interesting layers.

Potential Areas for Improvement:
- The opening scene at the cemetery feels a bit exposition-heavy in conveying the backstory. Finding a more visual/subtle way to set up the character's grief could be more cinematic.
- The transition from Jay rejecting therapy to immediately going to a dispensary feels a bit abrupt. Adding a few more beats to that character choice could smooth it out.
- The final scene introducing a random new character (Mark) seems a bit out of place. It may work better to keep that scene between just the two friends.
- Overall, fleshing out the character arcs and emotional journeys a bit more could elevate the depth of the narrative.

But there is definitely strong potential here for an impactful, emotionally resonant short film that explores profound themes of grief, identity, mental health, and perseverance in an authentic way. The keymay be striking the right balance between the dramatic emotional core and the slice-of-life realism in the dialogue/vignettes.

[ASSISTANT] 2024-05-17T01:46:34.527581Z
 I have the same overall thoughts on the script as before. The premise of exploring grief, mental health struggles, and finding purpose through the lens of these characters has strong potential for an emotionally resonant short film.

Some additional thoughts:

Positives:
- The naturalistic dialogue between Jay and Frank feels authentic and captures the dynamic of close friends well.
- Touching on issues like therapy vs. self-medicating with cannabis raises interesting moral/ethical questions in a non-judgmental way.
- Small character details like Frank pursuing creative dreams in Hollywood add nice texture.

Potential Areas for Improvement:
- The transition from rejecting therapy to immediately going to the dispensary still feels a bit abrupt in Jay's character arc. Adding a beat or two more motivation could smooth it.
- While the scene at the restaurant is slice-of-life realistic, introducing a new character (Mark) that late feels somewhat extraneous to the core story.
- The ending is quite open-ended. While ambiguous endings can work, providing a few more narrative threads or character resolutions could give it a stronger sense of dramatic completeness.

Overall, I think there is a really compelling foundation here for an exploratory character study that tackles heavy themes authentically and without sensationalism. Honing in on the core emotional through-lines and character arcs could elevate it from an interesting vignette-style script to a truly impactful and resonant short film. The keychallenges seem to be finding the balance between the dramatic weight and the naturalistic lightness in both the plotting and dialogue.
