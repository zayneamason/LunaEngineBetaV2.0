 To properly display a front license plate in Los Angeles County, California, you need to follow these guidelines:

1. California law requires vehicles to display both front and rear license plates.

2. The front license plate should be securely fastened to the front of the vehicle.

3. It should be mounted in a bracket specifically designed for license plates.

4. The plate must be clearly visible and not obscured by any part of the vehicle or accessories.

5. It should be mounted horizontally (not vertically or at an angle).

6. The plate should be clean and legible from a distance.

7. No covers or frames that obstruct any part of the license plate information are allowed.

8. The plate must be mounted outside the vehicle (not inside the windshield or on the dashboard).

9. For most passenger vehicles, the bottom of the license plate should be at least 12 inches from the ground.

Failure to properly display a front license plate in Los Angeles County can result in a fix-it ticket or a fine. If your vehicle didn't come with a front license plate bracket, you may need to have one installed.
