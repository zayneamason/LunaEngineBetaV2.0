# Module 4: Confidentiality and Intellectual Property Agreement

This Confidentiality and Intellectual Property Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE], by and between:

Shalley [Last Name], residing at [ADDRESS] ("Producer A")

and

Derrick [Last Name], residing at [ADDRESS] ("Producer B")

Collectively referred to as the "Parties" or individually as a "Party."

WHEREAS, the Parties are engaged in the co-production of a fashion show event titled "[EVENT NAME]" (the "Event") and will exchange confidential and proprietary information in the course of their collaboration;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the Parties agree as follows:

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

1.1 "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, either directly or indirectly, in writing, orally, or by inspection of tangible objects, relating to the Event, including but not limited to:

   a) Business plans, financial information, and marketing strategies
   b) Creative concepts, designs, and production plans
   c) Sponsor and investor information
   d) Client, vendor, and talent relationships
   e) Technical specifications and production techniques
   f) Any other information that should reasonably be recognized as confidential information of the disclosing Party

1.2 Confidential Information shall not include information that:

   a) Was publicly known prior to the time of disclosure
   b) Becomes publicly known through no fault of the receiving Party
   c) Is obtained from a third party without breach of any obligation of confidentiality
   d) Is independently developed by the receiving Party without use of the disclosing Party's Confidential Information

## 2. Non-Disclosure and Non-Use

2.1 Each Party agrees to:

   a) Maintain the confidentiality of the other Party's Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own confidential information, but no less than a reasonable degree of care
   b) Not disclose such Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party
   c) Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of planning and executing the Event
   d) Limit access to the Confidential Information to its employees, contractors, and agents who have a need to know such information for the purpose of the Event and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those in this Agreement

2.2 Each Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by law, provided that the receiving Party gives the disclosing Party prompt written notice of such requirement prior to disclosure and assists in obtaining an order protecting the information from public disclosure.

## 3. Intellectual Property Rights

3.1 Each Party retains all rights, title, and interest in and to its own pre-existing intellectual property.

3.2 Any intellectual property jointly created by the Parties in the course of planning and executing the Event shall be jointly owned by the Parties, including but not limited to:

   a) The Event name and logo
   b) Marketing materials and promotional content
   c) Event concept and production design
   d) Custom software or technical solutions developed for the Event

3.3 Each Party grants the other a non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use jointly owned intellectual property for the purpose of promoting and executing the Event.

3.4 Both Parties retain the right to use photos and videos from the Event for their own promotional purposes, subject to any agreed-upon restrictions.

## 4. Return of Confidential Information

Upon the disclosing Party's request or upon termination of the Parties' collaboration, the receiving Party shall:

a) Return all tangible materials containing Confidential Information
b) Permanently delete or destroy all electronic copies of Confidential Information
c) Certify in writing that all such materials have been returned, deleted, or destroyed

## 5. Term and Termination

5.1 This Agreement shall remain in effect until the completion of the Event and all related financial matters are settled, unless terminated earlier by mutual agreement of both Parties.

5.2 The obligations of confidentiality under this Agreement shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

## 6. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm and that monetary damages may be inadequate. In the event of a breach, the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 7. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of California. Any disputes arising from this Agreement will be resolved in the state or federal courts located in [County], California.

## 8. Entire Agreement and Amendments

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements relating to its subject matter. Any modifications must be made in writing and signed by both Parties.
