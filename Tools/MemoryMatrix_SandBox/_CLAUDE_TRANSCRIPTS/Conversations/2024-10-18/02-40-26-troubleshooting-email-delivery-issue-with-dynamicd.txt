 DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) authentication failure is a common email issue that can affect your domain's email deliverability. Let's address this problem:

1. Understand DKIM:
   DKIM is an email authentication method that adds a digital signature to your outgoing emails, allowing receiving servers to verify that the email truly came from your domain and wasn't altered in transit.

2. Check DKIM configuration:
   - Log into your email service provider's control panel
   - Look for DKIM or email authentication settings
   - Ensure DKIM is enabled for your domain

3. Verify DKIM record:
   - Your email provider should give you a DKIM record to add to your DNS
   - This record usually looks like a TXT record with a specific name (selector) and a long string value

4. Add/Update DNS record:
   - Log into your DNS management interface (often at your domain registrar)
   - Add or update the TXT record for DKIM
   - The record name should be something like: selector._domainkey.yourdomain.com
   - Paste the entire DKIM value provided by your email service

5. Wait for propagation:
   - DNS changes can take up to 48 hours to propagate fully
   - Wait at least a few hours before testing again

6. Test DKIM configuration:
   - Use online DKIM checker tools to verify your setup
   - Send a test email to a service like mail-tester.com for a full report

7. Check for formatting issues:
   - Ensure there are no extra spaces or line breaks in your DKIM record
   - Some DNS interfaces require you to enclose the DKIM value in quotation marks

8. Multiple selectors:
   - If you're using multiple email services, you may need multiple DKIM records
   - Ensure each has a unique selector name

9. Key rotation:
   - If you've recently rotated your DKIM keys, make sure the DNS is updated with the new key

10. Consult your email provider:
    - If you're still having issues, contact your email service provider
    - They can provide specific guidance for their DKIM implementation

11. Check sending servers:
    - Ensure all servers sending email on behalf of your domain are properly configured for DKIM

Remember, DKIM is just one part of email authentication. You should also ensure SPF (Sender Policy Framework) and DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance) are properly set up for best email deliverability.
