## On Senator Bernard Sanders' Address on Trump's Authoritarianism
*January 2026*

*First President, Commander-in-Chief*

*He walks to the window*

"Let me be direct about what I hear in this transcript. This president has declared that his own morality is 'the only thing that can stop him.' That he does not need international law. That he threatens war against Denmark—a NATO ally—to seize territory."

*Turns back to face the council*

"Gentlemen, I refused a crown. I walked away from power *twice* when the nation begged me to stay. I did so because I understood what unlimited power does to men. I watched officers in my own army scheme for personal advantage the moment victory was secured. Human nature does not change."

"The Senator lists the actions: withholding Congressional appropriations, defying court orders, investigating political opponents, renaming federal buildings after himself, sending masked agents to drag citizens from their cars, threatening military occupation of American cities. Each of these alone would have constituted grounds for revolution in 1776. Together?"

*Sits heavily*

"I designed the precedents of the presidency to constrain precisely this—a man who believes his own mind is the only limit on his power. What the Senator describes is the unraveling of everything I spent eight years building."

"But what troubles me most? The Senator says Republicans are only *now* understanding the folly. Only *now*. After all of this. The factions I warned against have produced precisely the 'alternate domination' and 'spirit of revenge' I predicted would be a 'frightful despotism.' **Washington's Bottom Line:** "This is not governance. This is what we fought a revolution to escape."
