[ASSISTANT] 2024-03-07T23:54:59.453589Z
 There is no definitive date for when humans first started keeping animals as pets, but the practice likely originated tens of thousands of years ago. Here's a brief overview:

- Dogs were likely the first domesticated animals, descended from wolves. Archaeological evidence suggests dogs may have been domesticated between 20,000-40,000 years ago.

- Cats were among the next animals domesticated, with archaeological data indicating cat domestication occurring around 9,500-4,000 years ago in the Middle East.

- Other animals like goats, sheep, pigs, cattle and horses were first domesticated for agricultural purposes starting around 9,000-11,000 years ago, but were later also kept as pets.

- Evidence of pets like domesticated birds, mice, rabbits and guinea pigs dates back over 5,000 years in places like Egypt, Greece and Rome.

So while the lines between working animal and pet were blurred early on, there is evidence humans have been keeping animal companions just for companionship purposes for at least 10,000 years, with the human-dog bond going back even further into prehistoric times.

 Tortoises are among the earliest known pet animals kept by humans. There is evidence that tortoises were kept as pets dating back thousands of years:

- Tortoises were recorded as being given as gifts among nobility in ancient China around 2500 years ago.

- Mediterranean tortoises were likely first domesticated as pets by the Phoenicians or ancient Greeks and Romans in the 8th century BC or earlier.

- Giant tortoises from the Indian Ocean islands like the Seychelles were taken aboard ships as pets and food by sailors as early as the 16th century.

So tortoises have an extremely long history of being kept by humans as pets and companions, with the practice dating back at least 4,000-5,000 years across various ancient civilizations around the world. Their longevity and ease of care made them desirable pets throughout antiquity.
