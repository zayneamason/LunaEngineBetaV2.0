Here is a suggested folder structure for your Google Drive that covers your photography/videography business, personal files, and career portfolio:

My Drive
- Business
   - Clients
      - Client 1
         - Contracts & Invoices
         - Receipts
         - Session Media
      - Client 2
      - Client 3
   - Marketing Materials
   - Expenses & Accounting
- Personal
   - Finance & Taxes
   - Photos & Videos
   - Documents
   - Other
- Career
   - Resume & CV
   - Portfolio
      - Photography
         - Galleries  
            - Weddings
            - Portraits 
            - Landscapes
         - Best of...
      - Videography 
         - Music Videos
         - Events
         - Reels
   - Certificates & Training
   - Job Applications

The Business folder contains all your client work, contracts, invoices and everything business related. Separate each client into their own folder.

The Personal folder keeps your personal life organized with taxes, documents, media and other files.

The Career folder stores your up-to-date resume, portfolio galleries for your best work, training certificates, and job application material.

The portfolio subfolders allow you to organize projects by type and create collections of your best work to showcase your abilities to potential clients or employers.

Let me know if you would like any clarification or have additional questions!

[ASSISTANT] 2023-11-30T09:11:03.150255Z
Here are some suggested tags that could help enhance the organization within the recommended Google Drive folder structure:

Business:
- Client Name
- Project Name 
- Date (of shoot, contract signing, etc)
- Location  
- Type (photo shoot, video shoot, wedding, etc.)

Personal:
- Taxes - Year (e.g. 2023) 
- Finance - Year 
- Photos - Date, Location, Event/Subject
- Videos- Date, Location, Event/Subject

Career:
- Portfolio - Subject (Wedding, Portrait, Landscape, Reel, etc)
- Resume - Role (Graphic Designer, Photographer, Videographer)
- Certifications - Name, Year Earned  
- Applications - Company, Position, Year Applied

Using clear, descriptive tags related to clients, projects, dates, and subject matter can enable easier search and organization. Tags like location, event type, portfolio gallery, job title can help surface context quickly.

Standardized tags by year and date ranges aid chronological organization and retrieval of older content.

Implementing a thoughtful hierarchy and tagging conventions will maximize the effectiveness of this folder system over time. Let me know if any other tag examples would be helpful!
