what about more common rare coins?

[ASSISTANT] 2024-09-24T01:54:45.667900Z
 Certainly! There are many rare coins that are more common and potentially attainable for collectors. These coins are valuable but not nearly as scarce as the extremely rare specimens we discussed earlier. Here are some examples:

1. Indian Head Cent (1859-1909): Often found in old coin collections or antique stores.

2. Buffalo Nickel (1913-1938): Can sometimes be found in circulation or coin shops.

3. Mercury Dime (1916-1945): Occasionally found in old change jars or at flea markets.

4. Standing Liberty Quarter (1916-1930): Less common in circulation but available at coin shops.

5. Walking Liberty Half Dollar (1916-1947): Often available at coin dealers or shows.

6. Morgan Silver Dollar (1878-1904, 1921): Commonly found at coin shops and shows.

7. Peace Silver Dollar (1921-1935): Available at many coin dealers.

8. Wheat Pennies (1909-1958): Still occasionally found in circulation.

Where to look for these coins:

1. Coin shops: Most cities have at least one coin shop where you can buy these coins.

2. Coin shows: These events bring together dealers and collectors.

3. Online marketplaces: Websites like eBay or specialized coin selling sites.

4. Bank rolls: You might find wheat pennies or other older coins by searching through bank rolls.

5. Flea markets and antique stores: Sometimes you can find old coins here.

6. Metal detecting: With proper permissions, you might find old coins in the ground.

7. Inheritance or old collections: Family members might have old coin collections.

Remember, when collecting coins:

- Learn about proper coin handling and storage to preserve their value.
- Be aware of counterfeits, especially when buying online.
- Consider getting valuable coins authenticated by a reputable grading service.
- Start with more affordable coins and gradually work your way up as you learn more.
