[ASSISTANT] 2024-09-24T19:50:12.075610Z
 While NordVPN and proxy servers share some similarities in that they both can mask your IP address and route your traffic through another server, they are not exactly the same thing. Let me clarify the differences:

1. Proxy Server:
   - Routes your traffic through a single intermediary server
   - Usually only works for specific applications or protocols (e.g., web browsing)
   - Generally does not encrypt your traffic
   - Typically easier to set up but offers less protection

2. VPN (like NordVPN):
   - Routes all your internet traffic through an encrypted tunnel
   - Works for all applications and protocols on your device
   - Encrypts your data, providing more security and privacy
   - Usually requires software installation but offers more comprehensive protection

So while NordVPN does act as an intermediary between you and the internet (similar to a proxy), it's more accurate to call it a VPN service rather than a proxy server. VPNs like NordVPN offer more robust features and security measures compared to traditional proxy servers.

That said, some VPN providers, including NordVPN, do offer proxy services as part of their package. These might be used for specific purposes like quick IP masking for web browsing. However, when people refer to using NordVPN, they're typically talking about using its full VPN service, not just its proxy features.
