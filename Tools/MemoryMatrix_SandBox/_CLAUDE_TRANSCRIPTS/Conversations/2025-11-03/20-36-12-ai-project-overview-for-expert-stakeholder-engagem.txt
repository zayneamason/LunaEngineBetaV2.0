## TIER 1: Federal Reserve / Central Bank Tools
*These are the most powerful and can be deployed relatively quickly*

### **1. AI-Specific Reserve Requirements**

**What it is**: Banks must hold higher capital reserves against loans to AI companies or AI infrastructure projects

**How it works**:
- Base reserve requirement: 10%
- AI startup loans: 15% 
- AI infrastructure loans >$1B: 20%
- Adjusts automatically based on sector valuation metrics

**Which balloon**: VC/startup funding, infrastructure capex

**Implementation timeline**: 6-12 months (Fed rulemaking)

**Precedent**: Already done for real estate (higher reserves for mortgages), can extend to sector-specific

**Political difficulty**: Low - Fed has authority, technical not political

---

### **2. Countercyclical Capital Buffers (AI-Targeted)**

**What it is**: Banks must hold MORE capital when AI sector valuations are extreme, LESS when reasonable

**The Formula**:
```
Buffer_requirement = Base_rate × (Current_AI_Market_Cap / GDP) / Historical_average

Example:
- Historical average AI as % of GDP: 3%
- Current: 15% (5× historical)
- Buffer multiplier: 5×
- If base rate is 2%, buffer becomes 10%
```

**Which balloon**: All public AI company valuations

**Implementation**: Quarterly adjustments, announced in advance

**Precedent**: Basel III already has countercyclical buffers, just needs sectoral application

**Political difficulty**: Low - existing framework, technical adjustment

---

### **3. Fed Market Intervention (Direct Dragon-Slaying)**

**What it is**: Federal Reserve directly buys/sells AI stocks to stabilize markets

**Phase 1 - Build Inventory (NOW)**:
- Fed accumulates $50-100B in AI stocks
- Announced as "market stabilization program"
- Buys during normal/low valuations

**Phase 2 - Countercyclical Selling (when bubble metrics trigger)**:
- Automatic trigger: When AI sector >20% of S&P 500
- Fed sells $5-10B/month until <15%
- Proceeds go to Treasury (reduce debt)

**Which balloon**: Public company valuations (NVIDIA, Microsoft, etc.)

**Implementation**: Requires Congressional authorization (like QE did)

**Political difficulty**: Medium-High - controversial but has precedent in 2008

**Key advantage**: Direct, powerful, visible

## TIER 2: Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)

### **4. Progressive Margin Requirements**

**What it is**: How much investors can borrow to buy stocks varies by valuation

**The Mechanism**:
```
If Price/Earnings ratio:
- <25: 50% margin allowed (current standard)
- 25-50: 40% margin allowed
- 50-100: 30% margin allowed  
- >100: 20% margin allowed

For AI stocks with no earnings:
- Immediate 30% margin requirement
- Reduces to 40% once profitable for 2 years
```

**Which balloon**: Retail investor mania, momentum trading

**Implementation**: SEC rule change, 3-6 months

**Political difficulty**: Low - SEC has clear authority

**Effect**: Makes it harder/more expensive to speculate on extreme valuations

---

### **5. Position Limits on AI Stocks**

**What it is**: Individuals can only hold so much of a single AI stock

**The Mechanism**:
- Retail investors: Max 20% of portfolio in single AI stock
- Max 40% of portfolio in AI sector total
- Institutions: Max 10% in single name
- Enforced via broker reporting

**Which balloon**: Concentrated retail speculation

**Implementation**: SEC rulemaking, 6-12 months, phased in

**Political difficulty**: Medium - "limits freedom" but protects retail

**Precedent**: Already exists for commodities futures, extend to equities

---

### **6. Enhanced Disclosure Requirements**

**What it is**: AI companies must disclose much more about profitability, energy use, and capability claims

**Specific Requirements**:
- Quarterly: Revenue per inference, gross margin by product
- Annually: Total energy consumption, compute efficiency
- Immediately: Material capability limitations or failures
- Penalty: Delisting for material misstatements

**Which balloon**: Unrealistic valuations based on hype vs reality

**Implementation**: SEC rulemaking, effective 12 months after passage

**Political difficulty**: Low - literally just transparency

**Effect**: Reality check on valuations when data is public

## TIER 3: Treasury Department / Congress

### **7. Progressive AI Valuation Tax**

**What it is**: Tax on AI company market capitalizations that increases with total sector size

**The Formula**:
```
Tax applies to market cap exceeding $10B:

Tier 1 ($10-50B): 0.5% annual tax on excess
Tier 2 ($50-100B): 1.0% annual tax on excess  
Tier 3 ($100-500B): 2.0% annual tax on excess
Tier 4 (>$500B): 3.0% annual tax on excess

Sector trigger: If total AI market cap >15% of GDP, all rates double
```

**Example**: 
- NVIDIA at $3T market cap
- Pays: 0.5% × $40B + 1% × $50B + 2% × $400B + 3% × $2,450B
- = $200M + $500M + $8B + $73.5B = **$82.2B/year tax**

**Which balloon**: Mega-cap AI valuations

**Implementation**: Requires Congressional legislation, 12-24 months

**Political difficulty**: High - "wealth tax" controversy

**Effect**: Natural ceiling on valuations, funds social programs

---

### **8. AI Infrastructure Capex Surtax**

**What it is**: Additional tax on data center / AI compute capital expenditures

**The Mechanism**:
```
Normal corporate capex: Deductible as usual

AI compute capex:
- First $1B/year: Fully deductible  
- $1-5B/year: 75% deductible (25% surtax)
- $5-20B/year: 50% deductible (50% surtax)
- >$20B/year: 25% deductible (75% surtax)
```

**Which balloon**: Infrastructure capex bubble ($300B/year)

**Implementation**: Tax code change, Congress, 12-18 months

**Political difficulty**: Medium - framed as "responsible scaling incentive"

**Effect**: Makes incremental spending increasingly expensive

---

### **9. Carried Interest Elimination (AI-Specific)**

**What it is**: End the tax loophole for AI VC funds

**Current**: VC profits taxed at 20% capital gains rate
**Proposed**: AI VC profits taxed as ordinary income (37% rate)

**Exemption**: If fund holds investments >5 years, keeps capital gains treatment

**Which balloon**: Short-term speculation in AI startups

**Implementation**: Tax code, Congressional legislation

**Political difficulty**: Medium - politically popular but industry opposes

**Effect**: Incentivizes longer-term investment, discourages flipping

## TIER 4: Banking Regulators (OCC, FDIC)

### **10. Loan-to-Value Restrictions**

**What it is**: Limits on how much banks can lend against AI assets

**Commercial Real Estate for Data Centers**:
- Standard commercial: 80% LTV
- AI data centers: 60% LTV
- Adjusts to 50% if sector valuation >15% GDP

**Collateral Rules**:
- AI company stock: Max 50% LTV (vs 70% for broad market)
- Pre-revenue AI startup equity: 0% LTV (no lending against it)

**Which balloon**: Leveraged infrastructure development

**Implementation**: OCC/FDIC guidance, 6-12 months

**Political difficulty**: Low - bank safety is uncontroversial

---

### **11. Stress Test Scenarios (AI-Specific)**

**What it is**: Banks must prove they can survive AI bubble pop

**Annual Test Includes**:
- "AI market cap drops 60% over 18 months"
- "Major AI company bankruptcy"  
- "AI infrastructure becomes stranded assets"
- Must maintain capital ratios under all scenarios

**Effect**: Forces banks to limit AI exposure preemptively

**Which balloon**: All - through banking channel

**Implementation**: Stress test methodology update, annual cycle

**Political difficulty**: Low - "safety and soundness"

## TIER 5: International Coordination

### **12. G20 AI Valuation Agreement**

**What it is**: Coordinated intervention triggers across major economies

**The Compact**:
- All G20 nations implement countercyclical AI buffers
- Trigger levels agreed in advance (e.g. >15% of GDP)
- No country can offer "regulatory arbitrage"
- Annual monitoring and adjustment

**Why This Matters**: 
- Prevents "race to bottom"
- Companies can't jurisdiction shop
- Coordination prevents prisoner's dilemma

**Implementation**: Treaty negotiation, 24-36 months

**Political difficulty**: Very High - sovereignty concerns

**Critical**: Without this, companies just move to least-regulated jurisdiction
