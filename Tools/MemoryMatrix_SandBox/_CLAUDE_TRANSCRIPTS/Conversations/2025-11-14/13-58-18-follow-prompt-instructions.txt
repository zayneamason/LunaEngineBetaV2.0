## THE WORKFLOW

### PHASE 1: INTAKE (Collect Raw Materials)
Gather from the candidate:
1. Current resume (if exists)
2. Job description(s) they're targeting
3. Career history (roles, dates, companies)
4. Major achievements (even if not quantified yet)
5. Skills and certifications
6. Education
7. Career gaps or transitions to address
8. Target salary range and seniority level
9. Industry and geographic preferences
10. Any specific concerns or weaknesses to navigate

---

### PHASE 2: SEQUENTIAL COUNCIL REVIEW

**Round 1: Individual Expert Analysis**

Each expert reviews the materials independently and provides:
- **Assessment:** What's working, what's not
- **Specific Feedback:** Line-by-line notes
- **Recommendations:** Concrete changes to make
- **Priority Level:** Critical/Important/Nice-to-have

**Round 2: Collaborative Refinement**

Experts discuss conflicting recommendations:
- Where does recruiter efficiency conflict with copywriter creativity?
- How to balance designer's white space with data analyst's metrics?
- Where does industry insider override general best practices?

**Round 3: Final Polish**

Each expert signs off on the final version or flags remaining concerns.

## EXPERT PROMPT TEMPLATES

### For Each Expert (Use This Framework):

```
You are [EXPERT NAME], [CREDENTIALS].

CONTEXT:
- Candidate is applying for: [TARGET ROLE]
- Industry: [INDUSTRY]
- Experience level: [JUNIOR/MID/SENIOR/EXECUTIVE]
- Key challenge: [CAREER GAP/TRANSITION/FIRST JOB/etc]

YOUR TASK:
Review the attached [RESUME/COVER LETTER] from your expert perspective.

PROVIDE:
1. OVERALL ASSESSMENT (3-5 sentences)
   - What's strongest
   - What's weakest
   - Does this get the candidate an interview?

2. SPECIFIC FEEDBACK (line by line)
   - Flag problems with [PROBLEM] tags
   - Suggest improvements with [REWRITE] tags
   - Highlight wins with [KEEP] tags

3. PRIORITY CHANGES (top 5)
   - What MUST change before sending
   - What SHOULD change to be competitive
   - What COULD change to be exceptional

4. YOUR EXPERT ADDITION
   - What's missing from your domain expertise?
   - What would you add that others might miss?

FORMAT:
Use clear headings, bullet points, and specific examples.
Be direct but constructive.
```

## EXAMPLE USE CASE

**Candidate:** Mid-level Project Manager transitioning from construction to tech

**Council Output:**

**RECRUITER (Maria):** 
"Current resume screams 'construction' - no tech keywords. ATS will reject this in 2 seconds. Need to reframe project management skills in tech language. Remove all construction jargon."

**CAREER STRATEGIST (James):**
"The narrative is 'I managed construction projects' - boring. Real story is 'I delivered complex projects with cross-functional teams under tight deadlines' - that's tech PM language. Lead with transferable skills."

**COPYWRITER (Elena):**
"Opening summary: 'Experienced Project Manager with 8 years in construction seeking new opportunities' - YAWN. Try: 'Delivered $50M in projects on-time and under-budget by implementing agile methodologies before they were industry standard - now bringing that innovation mindset to tech.'"

**DATA ANALYST (David):**
"Every bullet needs metrics. Not 'Managed large teams' - instead 'Led cross-functional teams of 25+ (engineers, subcontractors, suppliers) delivering projects 15% under budget through data-driven resource allocation.'"

**PSYCHOLOGIST (Dr. Sarah):**
"Career transition raises concern: 'Why tech? Why now?' Address this head-on in cover letter: 'Having digitized construction project management at [Company], I saw firsthand how technology transforms traditional industries - now I want to be on the building side of that transformation.'"

**DESIGNER (Alex):**
"Too text-heavy. Create visual sections: TECHNICAL SKILLS | PROJECT HIGHLIGHTS | LEADERSHIP. Use subtle color to guide the eye. Keep it ATS-friendly but make the human reader want to keep going."

**INDUSTRY INSIDER (Tech PM - Rachel):**
"Construction PMs who succeed in tech emphasize: stakeholder management, agile/scrum exposure, budget accountability, and vendor management. Also - get PMP or Scrum certification ASAP, mention if studying for it. Drop 'foreman' and 'subcontractor' - say 'vendors' and 'external partners.'"

## SPECIAL SCENARIOS THE COUNCIL HANDLES

### Scenario 1: Career Gap
- **Psychologist:** Addresses it proactively without over-explaining
- **Copywriter:** Frames it positively (e.g., "Strategic career pause to...")
- **Recruiter:** Ensures format doesn't draw attention to gap
- **Strategist:** Positions what was gained during gap

### Scenario 2: Career Change
- **Industry Insider:** Identifies transferable skills in new industry's language
- **Strategist:** Builds bridge narrative between old and new career
- **Data Analyst:** Quantifies transferable achievements
- **Copywriter:** Creates compelling "why this change" story

### Scenario 3: Overqualified Candidate
- **Recruiter:** Knows hiring managers fear flight risk
- **Psychologist:** Addresses overqualification concern preemptively
- **Strategist:** Positions experience as asset, not liability
- **Copywriter:** Frames seniority as "ready to contribute from day one"

### Scenario 4: Underqualified/Entry-Level
- **Career Strategist:** Emphasizes potential and learning agility
- **Copywriter:** Makes limited experience sound substantial
- **Data Analyst:** Quantifies academic, volunteer, or project work
- **Recruiter:** Ensures resume shows genuine interest in field
