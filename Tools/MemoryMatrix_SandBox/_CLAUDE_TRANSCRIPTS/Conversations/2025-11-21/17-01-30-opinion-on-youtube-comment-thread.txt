## What You Actually Discovered

### 1. The Dialogue Instructions Work
The critical innovation was this prompt engineering:

```
CRITICAL: You are in a CONVERSATION with [other system].
- RESPOND to their output directly
- Reference their points
- Make this a dialogue, not a monologue
```

Without this, you get parallel monologues (Option A).  
With this, you get **cognitive collaboration** (Option C).

### 2. Context Handoff ≠ Conversation
Early tests showed:
- System 1 sees System 2's output as context ✓
- But doesn't engage with it ✗

The fix was **explicit dialogue prompting**. You can't just pass context—you have to instruct the system to *respond* to it.

### 3. Chunking Granularity Matters
- 1 chunk = no conversation (just a monologue)
- 2 chunks = one exchange (proof of concept)
- 5 chunks = full multi-turn reasoning with position evolution

The sweet spot seems to be **4-6 chunks** for complex prompts.

### 4. Token Allocation Is Critical
- 300 tokens → cut off mid-thought
- 500 tokens → complete but might truncate synthesis
- 600 tokens → full arguments, natural conclusions

For production, you'd probably want **dynamic token allocation** based on chunk position (later chunks need more space for synthesis).
