[ASSISTANT] 2025-01-27T10:25:20.718539Z
 Yes! Relativity is a fundamental physics theory developed by Albert Einstein that describes how space, time, and gravity are interconnected. There are actually two main theories:

Special Relativity (1905):
- Shows that space and time are connected as "spacetime"
- Nothing can travel faster than light
- Time moves differently for objects moving at different speeds (time dilation)
- Mass and energy are equivalent (E = mc²)

General Relativity (1915):
- Explains gravity as a curvature of spacetime caused by mass/energy
- Massive objects like stars and planets warp the fabric of spacetime
- This warping is what we experience as gravity
- Predicted things like black holes and gravitational waves (which were later confirmed)

An everyday example of relativity in action is GPS satellites - they have to account for time moving slightly differently in orbit compared to Earth's surface to maintain accuracy!
