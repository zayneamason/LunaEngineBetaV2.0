Yes. Unquestionably, unambiguously, permanently yes.

**The Legal Foundation:**

1. **Duty of Care**: When government asks citizens to respond to emergencies, it assumes responsibility for consequences of that response. The first responders acted under governmental authority (FDNY, NYPD, Port Authority, EMS). Their injuries are service-connected.

2. **Breach of Contract**: The implicit contract of citizenship is reciprocal: citizens defend the nation, the nation defends the citizens. The first responders upheld their end. The government's failure to uphold its end constitutes breach.

3. **Estoppel**: The government cannot claim "it's not from the pile" after years of exposure, then later admit causation but deny responsibility. Having admitted causation, the government is estopped from denying obligation.

**The verdict is clear. So why hasn't remedy been provided?**

Because this isn't a legal problem—**it's a political problem dressed in legal language.**
