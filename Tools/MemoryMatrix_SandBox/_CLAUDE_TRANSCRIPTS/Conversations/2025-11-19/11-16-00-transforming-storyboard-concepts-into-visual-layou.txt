## **IMMERSIVE EXPERIENCES**

### 1. **"The Portal"**
A full-scale walk-through LED tunnel that transforms based on participant movement. 
- **Skill focus:** Parametric design, interactive lighting systems, spatial sequencing
- **Challenge:** How do you create illusion of infinite depth in finite space?
- **Deliverables:** Concept sketches → 3D model showing LED wall integration → CAD specs for LED grid + control systems → renders from multiple angles

### 2. **"Synesthesia Installation"**
A multi-sensory space where sound triggers architectural light patterns. Design a structure that translates audio frequencies into spatial/visual form.
- **Skill focus:** Lighting design, material response systems, technical coordination
- **Challenge:** How does structure physically respond to sound?
- **Deliverables:** Concept → mood board → 3D render showing material variations → technical specs for lighting + structure

---

## **LARGE-SCALE STAGE/VENUE**

### 3. **"Festival Ground Zero"**
Design a main stage for a 50K+ person festival that can transform between 3 different acts/vibes throughout the day without crew changeover.
- **Skill focus:** Modular design, sight lines, scalability, rapid transformation
- **Challenge:** Same structure, radically different looks using LED/projection/material repositioning
- **Deliverables:** Concept → 3D render (daytime config) → 3D render (nighttime config) → CAD showing modular elements → technical specs

### 4. **"The Inverted Pavilion"**
A stage structure where the audience is *above* and the performance happens in a sunken arena. Completely rethink traditional sightlines.
- **Skill focus:** Spatial problem-solving, sight line engineering, dramatic lighting
- **Challenge:** How do you light a pit stage? How does crowd feel immersed looking down?
- **Deliverables:** Concept → site plan showing elevation changes → 3D isometric rendering → section drawings → lighting diagram

---

## **MATERIAL/STRUCTURAL EXPLORATION**

### 5. **"Responsive Membrane"**
Design a facade using a material system that reacts to wind, temperature, or proximity. The structure itself is the visual element.
- **Skill focus:** Material research, kinetic design, sustainable materials
- **Challenge:** How do you specify a material that moves/changes in real-time?
- **Deliverables:** Mood board of material references → 3D model showing movement states → material close-ups → CAD specs + material callouts

### 6. **"Fractured Geometry"**
A stage structure built from tessellating/interlocking geometric shapes that create visual rhythm and sightline management. Inspired by parametric architecture.
- **Skill focus:** Parametric modeling, modular fabrication, spatial efficiency
- **Challenge:** How do individual geometric units create cohesive whole?
- **Deliverables:** Parametric 3D model → multiple angles → detail of intersection points → fabrication specs for each module

---

## **NARRATIVE/STORY-DRIVEN**

### 7. **"Decay → Rebirth"**
Design a single stage installation that *visually tells a story* through material states: pristine → weathered → overgrown → reconstructed.
- **Skill focus:** Material degradation, narrative design, lighting for mood progression
- **Challenge:** How does design evolution communicate without words?
- **Deliverables:** 4 rendering variations (one for each state) → mood board showing material evolution → CAD showing structural constants/material changes

### 8. **"The Threshold"**
Design an architectural gateway/portal that transitions visitors from the "mundane world" into a festival/immersive space. It should feel monumental.
- **Skill focus:** Spatial dramaturgy, scale perception, materiality
- **Challenge:** How do you create psychological/visual shift in 20 feet?
- **Deliverables:** Concept from participant POV → 3D rendering from approach angle → CAD elevation → detail of threshold edge

---

## **TECHNICAL/SYSTEMS-FOCUSED**

### 9. **"Modular LED Matrix"**
Design a reusable, reconfigurable LED wall system that can be deployed in different configurations: flat wall, curved surface, sculptural form, overhead canopy.
- **Skill focus:** Modular systems design, LED integration, structural flexibility
- **Challenge:** One design, infinite configurations
- **Deliverables:** Base unit 3D model → 3 deployment scenarios rendered → CAD showing connection points → spec sheet for modular elements

### 10. **"Sound-Reactive Architecture"**
A stage structure with built-in sound sensors that drive LED color/intensity shifts. Design the physical form AND the control system logic.
- **Skill focus:** Lighting design, technical systems, performance integration
- **Challenge:** How does the building "respond" authentically to sound?
- **Deliverables:** Concept → 3D render showing LED placement → technical diagram showing sensor/control system → renders showing different audio states

---

## **ENVIRONMENTAL/SITE-SPECIFIC**

### 11. **"Desert Bloom"**
Design a temporary installation for a desert environment that uses the landscape as part of the design. Integrates architecture with natural topography.
- **Skill focus:** Site planning, material adaptation, environmental integration
- **Challenge:** How do you design for harsh conditions while respecting the natural site?
- **Deliverables:** Site map → concept render from ground level → aerial render showing integration → material/engineering specs for desert durability

### 12. **"Vertical Festival"**
Reimagine a traditional horizontal festival footprint as a vertical experience. Multiple levels, sight lines, material transitions as you ascend.
- **Skill focus:** Vertical circulation, multi-level design, spatial hierarchy
- **Challenge:** How do you manage crowds, sight lines, and experience across Z-axis instead of X-Y?
- **Deliverables:** Site section showing elevation changes → multiple 3D perspectives from different levels → wayfinding diagram → structural engineering overview

---

## **ARTISTIC/CONCEPTUAL**

### 13. **"The Labyrinth"**
Design a walk-through structure where the *path itself* tells a story. Visitors make navigational choices that affect their experience. Walls, lighting, materials communicate meaning.
- **Skill focus:** Spatial narrative, wayfinding, material symbolism
- **Challenge:** How do you guide without being obvious? How does design language communicate without signage?
- **Deliverables:** Floor plan showing path variations → 3D render from inside labyrinth → concept sketches showing material/color progression → visitor journey diagram

### 14. **"Gravity Defied"**
Design a structure where gravity appears to work differently. Visually inverted elements, impossible angles, material arrangements that seem to float.
- **Skill focus:** Optical illusion, structural honesty (it's real but looks impossible), material contrast
- **Challenge:** How do you create wonder? How do you make something that's engineered look like it breaks physics?
- **Deliverables:** Concept sketches showing impossible geometry → 3D render from hero angle → CAD showing actual structure → detail shots highlighting the illusion

### 15. **"Temporal Shift"**
Design a space that *feels* like it exists in multiple time periods simultaneously. Blend brutalist concrete, sleek LED, organic growth, decay, renewal.
- **Skill focus:** Material mixing, aesthetic cohesion, narrative complexity
- **Challenge:** How do you combine contradictory aesthetics without it feeling chaotic?
- **Deliverables:** Mood board mixing era references → 3D render showing all elements → detail shots of era transitions → material spec callouts

## **HOW TO USE THESE PROMPTS:**

**Pick 1-2 that excite you.** Not because they're "safe"—because they make you think differently.

**For each:**
1. Sketch rough concepts (5-10 quick ideas)
2. Develop 1-2 strongest concepts fully
3. Build 3D model in Sketchup/Blender
4. Export technical CAD specs
5. Create 4-5 polished renders (different angles, lighting scenarios)
6. Write 1-paragraph design brief (challenge → my approach → what this proves)
7. Follow the 7-slide Google Slides format

---

**These prompts are "big" because:**
- They require systems thinking (not just making something pretty)
- They push a specific skill (LED, parametric, structural, narrative)
- They show ambition without being unrealistic
- They prove you can think like a production designer, not just an artist

**Pick the one that makes you say: "I want to solve this problem."**

That's the one to build.
