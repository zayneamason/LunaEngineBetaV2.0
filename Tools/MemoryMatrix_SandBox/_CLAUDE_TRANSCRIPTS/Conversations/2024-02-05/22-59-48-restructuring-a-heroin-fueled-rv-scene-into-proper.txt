 The hero's journey is a common narrative pattern found in many myths, legends, and stories across cultures. It was famously analyzed and articulated by American scholar Joseph Campbell in his book "The Hero with a Thousand Faces."

In summary, the hero's journey follows these major stages:

1. The Call to Adventure - The hero starts in their regular world and receives a call/prompt to go on an adventure or quest. They are reluctant at first.

2. Crossing the Threshold - The hero commits to the adventure and crosses into a new, unfamiliar world. 

3. Trials - The hero faces tests, challenges, temptations, and setbacks along their journey. They encounter allies and enemies.

4. The Abyss - The hero faces a major crisis or lowest point where they appear defeated. All seems lost.

5. Transformation - The hero undergoes a change or transformation, finding new skills or weapons to aid them.

6. Revelation - The hero has an epiphany, a deeper realization about themselves and the nature of the quest. 

7. The Climax - The hero faces and defeats their greatest challenge, often facing the Shadow or enemy.

8. The Return Home - The hero crosses back over the threshold, returns to their ordinary world, and brings back some wisdom, power or elixir that transforms themselves or society. 

The hero's journey underlies many myths, legends, folk tales, and popular stories across the world. Understanding this narrative pattern can help writers craft compelling hero story arcs. Many blockbuster movies and novels consciously utilize this structure.

 The hero's journey is a common narrative pattern found in many myths, legends, and stories across cultures. It was famously analyzed by scholar Joseph Campbell, who outlined its basic structure:

1. The Ordinary World: The hero exists in a mundane situation before receiving a call to adventure 

2. The Call to Adventure: The hero is presented with a problem, challenge, or adventure to undertake

3. Refusal of the Call: The hero initially hesitates or even refuses the call to adventure 

4. Meeting the Mentor: The hero encounters a mentor figure who provides wisdom, advice, training, magical gifts, or other assistance

5. Crossing the First Threshold: The hero commits to the adventure and leaves the ordinary world

6. Tests, Allies, Enemies: The hero faces trials that test him in various ways. He also meets allies and enemies. 

7. Approach to the Inmost Cave: The hero nears the central crisis of the adventure

8. The Ordeal: The hero faces a dangerous situation, often confronting death itself

9. The Reward: The hero seizes a reward after surviving death. This could be a magical elixir, sword, secret knowledge, etc. 

10. The Road Back: The hero must return home, but the journey will hold even more challenges for the hero

11. The Resurrection: The hero undergoes a final test where everything learned will be applied

12. Return with the Elixir: The hero returns with something to improve the ordinary world, bringing closure.

This journey mold has been identified in cultures worldwide. Writers and filmmakers still use it today to structure stories. The basic template allows infinite creative possibilities.
