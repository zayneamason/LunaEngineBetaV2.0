[ASSISTANT] 2024-12-13T21:08:22.908741Z
 Let me outline what's typically considered a more balanced, industry-standard approach to repairs and compliance:

Standard Middle Ground Approach:

1. Lessor Should Typically Handle:
- HVAC system maintenance and replacement
- Structural components
- Roof maintenance and repairs
- Building systems (main electrical, plumbing, etc.)
- Code compliance for base building
- ADA compliance for common areas and building exterior
- Any pre-existing code violations
- Capital improvements

2. Tenant Should Typically Handle:
- Interior cosmetic repairs
- Light bulb replacement
- Minor plumbing repairs (clogs, leaks in tenant-installed fixtures)
- Interior painting
- Carpet cleaning
- Their own trade fixtures
- ADA compliance specific to their use/improvements

3. Shared Responsibilities:
- HVAC: Often the tenant handles regular maintenance while landlord handles major repairs/replacement
- Windows/Doors: Tenant handles glass breakage, landlord handles frames and structural components
- Plumbing: Tenant handles minor repairs, landlord handles major systems

A More Balanced Version Would Include:

1. Modified HVAC Clause:
- Tenant maintains service contract
- Landlord responsible for replacement of units at end of useful life
- Tenant responsible for routine maintenance and minor repairs up to a reasonable cap (often $500-1,000)

2. Modified ADA Compliance:
- Landlord responsible for base building compliance
- Tenant only responsible for ADA requirements triggered by their specific use or improvements

3. Reasonable Repair Caps:
- Set a maximum dollar amount for any single repair the tenant is responsible for
- Typically $500-1,000 per occurrence
- Anything above that becomes landlord's responsibility

4. Clearer Definition of "Regular Maintenance" vs "Capital Improvements":
- Regular maintenance: Tenant
- Capital improvements: Landlord
- Clear useful life definitions (typically 3-5 years)

This current lease puts an unusually heavy burden on the tenant. A more balanced approach would protect both parties while ensuring proper maintenance of the property. Would you like me to provide specific language for any of these suggested modifications?
