## Base Compensation + Profit-Sharing Formula

A common approach is to establish:
1. Base compensation for each role
2. Profit-sharing percentages after expenses

For example:
- **You (Founder/Owner)**: Base salary + 40-50% of profits
- **Marketing Director**: Base salary + 15-20% of profits
- **Technical Director**: Base salary + 15-20% of profits
- **Artists/Designers**: Commission per design + royalty percentage (typically 3-7% of sales for specific designs)

## Revenue Allocation Formula

Another approach directly allocates revenue:
- 30-40% for business operations/growth
- 20-30% for owner compensation
- 10-15% for marketing director
- 10-15% for technical director
- 5-10% for artists/designers
- 5-10% for reinvestment fund

## Performance-Based Formulas

You could implement performance incentives:
- Base percentages as above
- Additional percentages when sales targets are met
- Quarterly bonuses based on growth metrics
- Higher royalties for best-selling designs

## Tiered Compensation Structure

As your business grows:
- Tier 1 (0-$100K revenue): Base percentages
- Tier 2 ($100K-$500K): Adjusted percentages with more for reinvestment
- Tier 3 ($500K+): Further adjusted percentages with increased operational allocation

## Considering Artist Compensation Options

For artists specifically:
- Flat fee per design (simplest)
- Flat fee + small royalty (balanced)
- Lower flat fee + higher royalty (incentivizes high-quality work)
- Escalating royalties for designs that exceed sales thresholds

## Important Considerations

When implementing these formulas:
- Document everything in your operating agreement
- Create clear definitions for "profit" (after which expenses?)
- Define payment schedules and calculation methods
- Include provisions for business changes or stakeholder exits
- Review regularly as the business evolves
