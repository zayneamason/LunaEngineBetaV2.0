Let me give a solid, actionable overview of biped robotics without being overwhelming. This is a big topic but I can break it down into the key considerations.Alright, biped robotics - ambitious as fuck but let's go.

**Core components you need:**

1. **Actuators** - Your joint motors
   - Servo motors (hobby-grade for small bots, Dynamixel for serious work)
   - Brushless motors with gearboxes (more power, more complexity)
   - Minimum 6 DOF per leg (hip: 3, knee: 1, ankle: 2)

2. **Structure**
   - Rigid frame (aluminum, 3D printed, carbon fiber)
   - Key: keep mass low and centered high (counterintuitive but helps balance)

3. **Sensors**
   - IMU (accelerometer + gyroscope) - absolutely critical for balance
   - Force sensors in feet (know when/how you're contacting ground)
   - Joint encoders (know where your limbs are)

4. **Control system**
   - This is where it gets spicy. Options:
     - **ZMP (Zero Moment Point)** - classic approach, keeps center of pressure in support polygon
     - **Inverted pendulum model** - simplifies the math
     - **Reinforcement learning** - train it in sim, transfer to real (Isaac Gym, MuJoCo)

**Starting points by budget/skill:**

- **Cheap learning:** Otto DIY (~$50, Arduino, walks like a drunk toddler but teaches fundamentals)
- **Mid-tier:** Build on Dynamixel servos + OpenCM controller
- **Serious:** Look at open-source platforms like **Open Dynamic Robot Initiative** or study Boston Dynamics patents

**The hard part** isn't mechanical - it's the control algorithms. Walking is a controlled fall.
