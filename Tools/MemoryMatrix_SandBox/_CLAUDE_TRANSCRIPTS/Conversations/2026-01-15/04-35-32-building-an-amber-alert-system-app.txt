Given his fabrication/tech background and project interests, he's probably asking about technical feasibility. Let me break this down practically.

AMBER Alert systems are actually government-coordinated through:
- Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA) - pushed via cell towers
- Emergency Alert System (EAS) - TV/radio
- NCMEC (National Center for Missing & Exploited Children) coordination

So the question is: what kind of app? Receiving alerts is straightforward. Issuing them requires law enforcement coordination and isn't something a random app can do.

Let me give him a direct, practical answer about what's technically possible and what the constraints are.Technically yes, but depends on what you mean by "AMBER Alert system":

**Receiving/displaying alerts** — Easy
- AMBER alerts push through Wireless Emergency Alerts (WEA) to all phones automatically via cell towers
- You could build an app that pulls from public feeds (NCMEC has an RSS/API), aggregates state DOJ feeds, or scrapes alert data
- Basically a news aggregator with filtering and notifications

**Issuing alerts** — Not really
- AMBER Alerts are coordinated through law enforcement → state coordinators → NCMEC → broadcast systems (WEA, EAS)
- You can't just... send one. It's a gated government system for obvious reasons

**Adjacent ideas that ARE possible:**
- Community alert/missing persons app (like Citizen but for missing kids specifically)
- Alert aggregator with better UX than what exists
- Tools for families to pre-stage info (photos, descriptions) for faster response if needed
- Volunteer coordination system for search efforts

- Meshtastic (open source LoRa mesh, already built for this)

**Hybrid approach:**
- Meshtastic for the backbone (works without any internet)
- Web dashboard for logging/coordination when connectivity exists
- Physical alert stations at key locations

Meshtastic is probably your fastest path to something real — it's literally designed for off-grid comms, supports alerts, and the hardware is cheap (~$30-50 per node).
