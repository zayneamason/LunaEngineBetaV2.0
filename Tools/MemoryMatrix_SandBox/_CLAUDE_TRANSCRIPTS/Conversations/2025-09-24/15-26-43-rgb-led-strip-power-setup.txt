 For 75ft of RGB LED strips, you'll need some solid power planning since that's quite a bit of lighting! Let me break down what you'll need:

**Power Supply:**
- Your strips are likely 12V or 24V (check the specs to confirm)
- At 96 LEDs/meter over 75ft (~23 meters), you're looking at roughly 2,200+ LEDs total
- RGB strips typically draw 14.4W per meter, so you'd need around 330W total
- I'd recommend getting a 400W power supply for headroom - something like a Mean Well LRS-400-12 (if 12V) or LRS-400-24 (if 24V)

**Controller:**
For that length, you'll want a controller that can handle the current load:
- **BTF-Lighting WS2811/WS2812B controller** (if your strips are addressable)
- **Gledopto GL-C-008P** (good RGB controller with app control)
- **Quinled-Dig2Go** (if you want something more advanced/DIY friendly)

**Important considerations:**
- You'll likely need power injection every 16-20 feet to prevent voltage drop
- Consider using multiple controllers/power supplies to split the load
- Make sure your controller's amp rating matches your total current draw

[ASSISTANT] 2025-09-25T01:56:49.325621Z
 For LED strip control with WiFi/Bluetooth on a MacBook, here are your best options:

**ESP32 (Highly Recommended):**
- **ESP32-WROOM-32 DevKit** or **ESP32-S3 DevKit**
- Built-in WiFi + Bluetooth
- Tons of GPIO pins for multiple LED strips
- Perfect for WLED firmware (amazing LED effects)
- Excellent Arduino IDE support on macOS
- Around $10-15

**Arduino with WiFi Shield:**
- **Arduino Uno R4 WiFi** - newest with built-in WiFi
- **Arduino MKR WiFi 1010** - compact with WiFi/Bluetooth
- More expensive than ESP32 ($25-40)

**Why ESP32 is ideal for your project:**
- **Native WLED support** - just flash the firmware and get incredible effects
- **Dual-core processor** handles LED calculations smoothly  
- **Multiple PWM channels** for complex setups
- **Web interface** accessible from any device
- **Sound reactive** capabilities with microphone add-on

**MacBook Setup:**
- Arduino IDE runs perfectly on macOS
- USB-C to micro-USB cable for programming
- CP2102 or CH340 drivers (usually auto-install)
- PlatformIO extension for VS Code (alternative IDE)

**For your 75ft LED project specifically:**
Get an **ESP32-WROOM-32** and flash it with **WLED firmware**. You'll have professional-level LED control with hundreds of effects, sound reactivity, and smartphone/web control - way more powerful than basic Arduino sketches.

The ESP32 + WLED combo is basically the gold standard for addressable LED projects right now.
