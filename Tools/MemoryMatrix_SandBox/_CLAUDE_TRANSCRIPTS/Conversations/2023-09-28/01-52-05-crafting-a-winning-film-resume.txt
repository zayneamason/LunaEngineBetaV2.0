what does a professional film resume look like?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ASSISTANT] 2023-09-28T01:52:23.814039Z
Here are some key elements that are typically included in a professional film resume:

- Name and contact information at the top (phone, email, website, etc.)

- A headshot in the top right corner. This should be a high quality professional headshot. 

- Lists of your film/TV credits - these are usually separated into different categories like "Film", "Television", "Commercials", etc. Under each category list the title of the project, the director, your role, and the year it was produced. List credits in reverse chronological order with your most recent work first.

- You can have sections for relevant skills like "Languages Spoken", "Special Skills" (things like stage combat, horseback riding, musical instruments, etc.), accents, etc.

- Sections for training and education like acting schools, workshops, or college degrees/training. 

- Memberships in relevant organizations like SAG-AFTRA.

- Summary or bio - This is optional but can be 2-3 sentences highlighting your experience and acting strengths at the bottom.

- The resume should ideally be 1 page and use a standard industry resume font and formatting. Resources like Actors Access provide templates.

- Avoid using first person language like "I" and write in concise, clear sentences.

- Only include your most relevant and best credits that showcase your abilities. Less is more on an acting resume.

The key is to showcase your acting experience in a clear, easy to read format for casting directors and agents. Highlight your strengths without embellishing.

I am a creative director, what does a professional resume for a creative director look like?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ASSISTANT] 2023-09-28T01:53:28.914936Z
Here are some key elements to include on a professional resume for a creative director:

- Contact information at the top (name, phone, email, website, LinkedIn)

- A summary or objective statement that briefly highlights your experience and skills as a creative director. This is usually 2-3 sentences.

- Work experience section - List your recent creative director roles in reverse chronological order. Include company name, position title, employment dates. Use bullet points to summarize your responsibilities and achievements in each role. Quantify results when possible.

- Skills section - List key skills like copywriting, art direction, leadership, creative strategy, project management, etc. You can organize them into categories or include a skills summary.  

- Software and tools - List design programs like Adobe Creative Suite, collaboration tools, CRMs or project management software you have experience with.

- Portfolio link - Include a link to your professional online portfolio showcasing your best creative work and campaigns. 

- Education - List any relevant degrees and certifications. No need to go back further than your Bachelor's degree.

- Honors/awards - Include any relevant honors and recognitions of your work.

- Volunteering or pro bono work in the field.

- Keep formatting clean, consistent and easy to scan. Use standard fonts like Arial, pt 11 or 12.

- Keep to 1-2 pages max.

The goal is to demonstrate your leadership skills, creative expertise, tools/software knowledge, and results from your work on major campaigns and projects.
