## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

### **Must-Do's:**

✅ **CREATE TEST SAMPLES**
- Make 2' x 2' sample with your exact process
- Test sand adhesion, color mixing, streak technique
- Verify fiber optic visibility through layers
- Practice finishing techniques
- **Budget $200-400 for test materials**

✅ **TEMPERATURE CONTROL**
- Epoxy cures best at 70-80°F
- Too cold = slow cure, potential cloudiness
- Too hot = rapid cure, cracking risk
- Climate-controlled shop environment essential

✅ **DUST-FREE ENVIRONMENT**
- Any dust becomes permanently embedded
- Seal work area, minimal air movement during cure
- Cover between pours

✅ **PERFECT LEVEL**
- Even 1° of tilt causes uneven epoxy thickness
- Use quality level, adjust substrate with shims
- Verify level before EVERY pour

✅ **BUBBLE MANAGEMENT**
- Torch is your friend (propane or butane)
- Pass torch 6-8" above surface to pop bubbles
- Don't overheat (can cause yellowing or burn)
- Work methodically, multiple passes

### **Common Mistakes to Avoid:**

❌ **Rushing Between Layers**
- Each layer must fully cure before next pour
- Impatience causes layer separation, cloudiness

❌ **Over-Pigmenting**
- Too much colorant = opaque epoxy
- Kills fiber optic effect
- Start light, add more color if needed in test samples

❌ **Ignoring Exotherm**
- Large epoxy pours generate significant heat
- Can crack or yellow if too hot
- Monitor with infrared thermometer, add fans if needed

❌ **Insufficient Mixing**
- Unmixed epoxy = soft spots or uncured areas
- Mix thoroughly for 3-5 minutes, scrape sides
- Consider drill mixer for large batches

❌ **Contamination**
- Don't touch curing epoxy (oils from skin)
- No water exposure during cure
- Keep pets/kids away from work area

With reduced depth, we need to adjust layer thicknesses:

### **Layer 1: Sand Base (Seal + Sand + Seal)**
**Depth:** ≈1/8" total

- Sand broadcast: single layer coverage
- Second seal coat: encapsulate sand
**Cure time:** 24 hours
**Material:** ~1 gallon epoxy + 8-10 lbs sand

### **Layer 2: Light Blue Base Pour**
**Depth:** 3/4" (half of total depth)
**Color:** Translucent light blue
**Purpose:** Main color layer, allows fiber optic light transmission

**Mixing Strategy:**
- Start with crystal clear epoxy
- Add blue pigment GRADUALLY (drops at a time)
- Test on white surface - should be translucent, not opaque
- Think "shallow Caribbean water" not "swimming pool"

**Amount needed:** ~12 gallons mixed epoxy
**Cure time:** 48-72 hours (thicker pour = longer cure)

**Technique:**
1. Mix epoxy thoroughly (5 minutes minimum)
2. Add pigment, stir 3 more minutes
3. Pour in spiral pattern from center outward
4. Use spreader to even thickness
5. Torch surface immediately (multiple passes)
6. Torch again after 10 minutes (new bubbles appear)
7. Torch once more after 30 minutes
8. Cover with plastic tent (dust protection)
9. Monitor for heat (shouldn't exceed 120°F)

---

### **Layer 3: Deep Blue Streak Layer**
**Depth:** 1/2"
**Color:** Darker blue, still somewhat translucent
**Purpose:** Create organic underwater depth effects

**Mixing Strategy - Two-Part Pour:**

**Part A: Clear base (8 gallons)**
- Crystal clear epoxy
- Very light blue tint (barely visible)

**Part B: Dark blue concentrate (2 gallons)**
- Saturated blue pigment
- Can be more opaque
- Will be poured in ribbons/streams

**Technique:**
1. Pour clear base layer evenly across surface
2. Immediately pour dark blue in organic patterns:
   - Curved lines
   - Scattered pools
   - Ribbon-like flows
3. Use heat gun or torch to manipulate:
   - Blow patterns gently to spread
   - Create feathered edges
   - Avoid over-mixing (want definition)
4. Work quickly (15-20 minute window)
5. Final torch pass for bubbles
6. Cover and cure

**Pattern Suggestions:**
- Follow fiber optic curves loosely (not exactly)
- More streaks on one side (asymmetry = natural)
- Vary streak width (1"-4" wide)
- Leave some areas lighter (represents shallows vs. deep)

**Cure time:** 48-72 hours

---

### **Layer 4: Clear Flood Coat (Final Surface)**
**Depth:** 1/4"
**Color:** Crystal clear
**Purpose:** Optical depth, smooth surface, protection

**Amount needed:** ~3 gallons mixed epoxy

**Technique:**
1. Mix crystal clear epoxy
2. Pour in zig-zag pattern
3. Self-levels naturally
4. Torch for bubbles
5. Cover immediately
6. **DO NOT TOUCH for 7 days minimum**

**Cure time:** 7-14 days to full hardness
