Unfortunately I don't have access to specific rate sheets or pricing for photography services. However, here are a few general tips on determining photography pricing:

- Consider your level of experience and skills. Photographers just starting out often charge less than established professionals. Review examples of your work to determine if you should charge entry level, mid-level or high-end rates.

- Factor in the type of photography. Weddings, portraits, events, commercial jobs etc. have different price points. More complex shoots like weddings tend to have higher rates. 

- Calculate your costs. Tally up gear expenses, software fees, travel/transportation, editing time, insurance etc. Price your work to cover costs and make a profit. 

- Research industry standards. Look at the going rates in your area for the types of photography services you want to provide. This gives you an idea of what clients expect to pay.

- Consider usage.Will the client only get digital images or want printing rights? Commercial usage costs more than personal use. Define the usage terms in your rate sheet.

- Have tiered pricing. Offer basic packages with rates for budget clients. Have premium packages with higher rates for more coverage, products or usage. 

- Don't undercharge. Consider the value of your time and talent. Price too low and you won't make a living. Price too high and you won't get clients. Set fair rates.
