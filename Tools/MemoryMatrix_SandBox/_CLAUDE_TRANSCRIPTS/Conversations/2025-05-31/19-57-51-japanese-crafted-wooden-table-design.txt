[ASSISTANT] 2025-05-31T19:59:21.059868Z
 Yes! An **infinity mirror** is an optical illusion created by placing two mirrors facing each other - one regular mirror and one two-way (or one-way) mirror - with LED lights positioned between them around the perimeter.

**How it works:**
- Light bounces back and forth between the mirrors
- Each reflection gets progressively dimmer
- Creates the illusion of an infinite tunnel of lights receding into the distance
- The two-way mirror allows you to see "into" the effect while the regular mirror reflects it back

**Common applications:**
- Art installations and museum exhibits
- Nightclub/bar decor
- Home accent pieces
- Retail displays
- Bathroom vanity mirrors (fancy versions)

**Key components:**
- Frame (often LED strip housing)
- Regular mirror (back)
- Two-way mirror (front, typically 70% reflective/30% transparent)
- LED strip lighting (usually programmable RGB)
- Power supply and controls
