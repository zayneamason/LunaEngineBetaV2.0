## Key Mathematical Insights

**1. The Doom Multiplier (L × P_AI)**
This term in the alignment equation grows fastest and matters most:
- At year 10: L×P_AI = 0.20 (manageable)
- At year 30: L×P_AI = 0.68 (dominant)
- At year 50: L×P_AI = 0.90 (overwhelming)

**2. Exponential vs Linear**
- S(t) grows exponentially (e^rt)
- L(t) grows logistically (sigmoid)
- M(t) grows exponentially (compound)
- Interventions provide linear pressure

**Result:** Without strong intervention, exponentials overwhelm linear corrections.

**3. The Lock-in Phase Transition**
L(t) doesn't increase linearly. It's a sigmoid curve:
- Slow initial growth (0.30 → 0.50 takes 15 years)
- Rapid middle growth (0.50 → 0.70 takes 5 years)
- Asymptotic approach to maximum (0.70 → 0.95 takes 20 years)

**Implication:** The critical period is years 15-25 when L(t) crosses 0.7.

**4. Compound Probability**
Survival probability compounds: P^N where N = years
- If P_year = 0.95 (95% per year)
- Then P_10years = 0.95^10 = 0.60 (60%)
- And P_50years = 0.95^50 = 0.08 (8%)

**Implication:** Even small annual risks accumulate to large long-term risks.

**5. Intervention Thresholds**
Each intervention has diminishing returns:
- 0 → 0.5: Large effect
- 0.5 → 0.8: Moderate effect
- 0.8 → 1.0: Small additional effect

**Implication:** Better to have all four interventions at 0.7 than two at 1.0 and two at 0.
