## Research Plan: Project Tapestry Funding Strategy

### Objective
Identify and prioritize funding sources for the Kinoni ICT Hub and broader Project Tapestry operations. Target: $70-125K Year 1.

---

### Phase 1: Validate Identified Leads (This Week)

**Lacuna Fund**
- Research: Current cycle timeline, eligibility criteria, past African language/cultural preservation grants
- Question to answer: Does cultural knowledge documentation qualify, or is it strictly ML datasets?

**Rotary Foundation Global Grants**
- Research: Application process, timeline, matching requirements
- Key advantage: Rotary Club of Kinoni provides built-in local partner requirement
- Question to answer: What's the realistic timeline from application to disbursement?

**Hewlett Foundation**
- Research: Current program priorities, whether Sunbird AI funding was one-off or programmatic
- Question to answer: Open RFP or invitation-only?

---

### Phase 2: Expand the Map (Week 2-3)

**Indigenous-focused funders**
- First Nations Development Institute (do they fund internationally?)
- Ford Foundation (Indigenous rights program)
- Christensen Fund (biocultural diversity focus)

**Tech sovereignty / digital rights angle**
- Mozilla Foundation
- Omidyar Network
- Digital Freedom Fund

**Uganda-specific**
- USAID Uganda programs
- UK FCDO (given Crane AI Labs' UK Gov AI Hub connection)
- African Development Bank digital initiatives

**Conference/convening support** (for ROSA track specifically)
- Skoll Foundation
- Rockefeller Foundation

---

### Phase 3: Strategic Questions to Resolve

1. **What's the ask structure?** Single comprehensive grant vs. stacked smaller grants for different components (solar, devices, cultural documentation, staff)?

2. **Who's the fiscal sponsor?** Do you have 501(c)(3) equivalency or need to partner with one?

3. **What's the Crane AI Labs angle?** Joint application possibility could strengthen tech credibility.

4. **What does Rotary Club of Kinoni actually need first?** Site assessment should precede detailed budget.
