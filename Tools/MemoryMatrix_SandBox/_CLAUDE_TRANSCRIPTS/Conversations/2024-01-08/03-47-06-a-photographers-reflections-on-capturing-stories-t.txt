Sitting in my car right now, I guess this will be a journal entry. As I continue my journey as a Photographer, I continuously happen to rethink and re-see everything - even stuff I've done in the past and all that. You know, I'm constantly asking myself questions for the purposes of self growth, but I don’t know what I'm trying to say. 

The big point of it all is one of the huge things I learned more recently is that business photography is really about relationship building, and that's like a huge part of what I think I missed out on - not really starting relationships, maintaining them, or whatever. But it's that the journey of photography, whatever it is, it's imperative that relationship building is its own skill set and it can be beautiful too. 

What I need to do to personally thrive in this industry is create valuable relationships with people. And those relationships will lead wherever they lead and strengthen my passion for photography. Maybe it's that way in any business. I guess we always have the option of not connecting with people like clients, but it probably makes things easier if we do. I don't necessarily want to be friends with everybody, but some people you just click with.

When it comes to personal growth and the passion and product I'm offering to the market, I think it's important that I embrace that relational part of the job and the people I'm working with, at least for that moment. I need to find my tribe - I need my people who believe in me, which I have found, but now it's expanding upon that and realizing the value.

There was another thing I was just thinking about for context... Last night I was at an anime event, and I love anime but I wasn't super passionate about it growing up. I wish I knew more but I don't want to be a poser - I don't know shit about anime! I was at this thing trying to be behind my camera, but the vibes or energy was off for me. It was all internal. 

It seemed like later people's energies changed and they were more inviting and talkative, but I think it's that I put my camera away for a bit. I was just in the moment - able to see people not through the lens or camera, not as objects, but as human beings having a good time, being fucked up, or just doing their thing. 

The camera can be a barrier that I throw up in front of myself for whatever reason. I guess the question I want to answer is - what are moments that are left uncaptured? Maybe not everything should be captured. What is capturing the moment? How do you know as a photographer when somebody captures a moment? When is the right time to be in the moment instead of capturing it?

It's that whole butterfly thing - don't chase the butterfly, let it come to you. Something my buddy told me about with women, that you shouldn't chase them, let them come to you. I think it's more applicable to photography. Moments are precious and as a photographer you want to capture them, but when are you taking away from the moment?

I take a shit ton of photos and then select the best ones later. But really amazing photographers will travel to the ends of the earth just to take one photo. It's the story behind the photo that makes it special, not that it's technically perfect. How do you capture the right moments while making sure you're part of the narrative?

Anyways, signing out.
