 For better fuel economy in a cargo van from around 2005, you might want to consider these alternatives:

1. Chevrolet Express 1500 with the smaller 4.3L V6 engine
- About 15-17 MPG city, 18-20 MPG highway
- Less power but significantly better on gas

2. Ford Econoline E-150 with the 4.6L V8
- About 14-16 MPG city, 17-19 MPG highway
- Good balance of utility and fuel economy

3. Dodge/Mercedes Sprinter (2004-2006)
- With the 2.7L diesel engine
- Around 20-25 MPG combined
- Best fuel economy in its class
- More expensive to buy and maintain, but saves on fuel

4. GMC Safari/Chevrolet Astro (through 2005)
- Smaller van overall
- 16-18 MPG city, 20-22 MPG highway
- Less cargo space but much better on gas

Keep in mind that if you don't need the heavy-duty 2500 capacity, going with a 1500 series van will generally give you better fuel economy due to the lighter construction and smaller engine options.

The diesel Sprinter would give you the best fuel economy, but they tend to be more expensive to purchase and maintain. If you're primarily concerned about fuel costs, it's worth considering whether you need a full-size van at all, as smaller commercial vans that came later (like the Ford Transit Connect) get much better gas mileage.
