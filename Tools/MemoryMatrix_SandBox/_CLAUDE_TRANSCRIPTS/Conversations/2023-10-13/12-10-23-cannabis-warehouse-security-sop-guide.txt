Here is a draft 1 page standard operating procedure guide for warehouse security in the cannabis industry:

Warehouse Security Standard Operating Procedures 

Objective: To maintain a secure cannabis warehouse facility and protect product, employees, and the community.

Access Control
- The warehouse perimeter shall be surrounded by an 8ft tall chainlink fence with barbed wire. 
- All warehouse access points shall have security cameras recording 24/7.
- Employees shall swipe keycards to access the warehouse and all access shall be logged. 
- Visitors shall be signed in and out at the front desk and be escorted by an employee.

Product Security
- Cannabis products shall be stored in a secure vault room with keycard and passcode access. 
- The vault shall have 24/7 video surveillance and motion sensors during closed hours.
- Daily product inventory shall be taken and compared to records in the seed-to-sale tracking system. 
- Products shall be packaged discretely and not advertised before leaving facility.

Personnel Safety  
- A panic button shall be installed at the front desk and other key locations.
- Emergency numbers shall be posted at the front desk and in the breakroom.
- Warehouse employees shall use the buddy system when working during closed hours.
- OSHA safety procedures shall be followed to avoid workplace hazards.  

Community Responsibility
- Loitering outside the warehouse shall be discouraged and local law enforcement alerted if needed.  
- Noise and odor shall be controlled per local regulations.
- Operate in a discreet manner to avoid attracting crime into the community.

Compliance
- All local and state rules and regulations shall be strictly followed. 
- Security policies shall be reviewed annually and updated as needed.
