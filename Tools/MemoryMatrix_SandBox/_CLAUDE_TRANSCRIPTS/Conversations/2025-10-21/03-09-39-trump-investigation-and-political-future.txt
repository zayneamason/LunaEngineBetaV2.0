## **Benjamin Franklin - Diplomat, Inventor, Pragmatist**

He believed in systematic approaches and organized governance, creating detailed schedules and methods for everything he did. The notion of a president who "didn't have a game, he was winging it" would strike Franklin as the antithesis of competent leadership.

Franklin's commitment to serving others and never patenting his inventions showed his belief that public service meant putting the common good above personal gain. Trump's explicit promises of "retribution" and using government power against political enemies would appall him. Franklin understood power's corrupting influence and believed leaders should use their positions to serve, not to settle scores.

The Project 2025 plan to stack government with ideological loyalists would concern Franklin greatly. He valued expertise, read voraciously, and believed in learning from the best minds. The idea of replacing experienced civil servants with "happy warriors" chosen primarily for loyalty over competence contradicts everything Franklin practiced. He would say: "In the affairs of government, experience and wisdom matter more than enthusiasm and allegiance to one man."

Most alarming to Franklin would be Trump's dismissal of intelligence briefings and his statement about encouraging Russia to attack NATO allies. As a diplomat who secured the French alliance that won American independence, Franklin understood that alliances and credibility are the foundation of national security.
