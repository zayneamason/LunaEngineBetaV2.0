## **George Washington - First President, Commander-in-Chief**

Speaker Johnson speaks of keeping the House in recess as "leverage." But where is the leverage in abandoning your post? A leader's place is at his post, especially during crisis.

The Speaker claims Democrats "chose to shut the government down." Perhaps. But **I learned in war that assigning blame does not feed soldiers or pay their wages**. By this logic, if I had won one battle, I could have retired to my farm and let someone else finish the war!

In my experience, **the man most focused on proving his patriotism is often the one most uncertain of it**. The whole point of the Constitution was to create a framework where competing interests **cancel each other out through negotiation, not paralysis**. Johnson has neutered his own institution.

Rome's Senate blocked grain shipments to starve political opponents. The Polish sejm required **unanimous consent**, giving each member a veto, which paralyzed the state.

We designed the Constitution to avoid these traps. We required **majority rule tempered by minority rights**. What Johnson has done is essentially create a **de facto veto** over government funding by refusing to negotiate. This is constitutional in form but **anti-constitutional in spirit**.

Let me make a broader point: The Constitution I helped write was designed to **force compromise through institutional friction**. The system works when:
1. The House passes a bill representing its majority
2. The Senate amends it representing ITS majority  
3. Differences are negotiated
4. Both chambers vote again
5. The President signs or vetoes
6. If vetoed, Congress can override

**Every step of this process requires actual engagement.** Johnson has violated the spirit of the system by withdrawing from it.

The fact that millions of citizens organized peaceful demonstrations to express opposition to government policies is **not** evidence of anti-American sentiment. My prescription is simple: **Return to constitutional process**. Recall the House. Begin negotiations.
