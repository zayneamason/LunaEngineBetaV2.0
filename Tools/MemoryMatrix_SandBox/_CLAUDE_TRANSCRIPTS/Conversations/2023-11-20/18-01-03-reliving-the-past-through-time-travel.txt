Here is a summary of the screenplay VoltecScript.pdf:

The screenplay centers around two friends named Don and Jerry who are dissatisfied with their lives. Jerry pays for them to use a service called Voltec that allows people to travel back in time and relive memories. However, they break the rules and try to change events in the past to improve their present lives. 

Initially it works - Don wakes up with a successful law career and Jerry as his partner. But Voltec catches on that they altered the timeline and forces them to go back again and undo their changes. Don and Jerry return to their normal unsatisfying lives but with a new perspective. The story explores themes of accepting one's present circumstances versus trying to recreate an idealized past. Ultimately Don decides to move forward in life instead of dwelling on what could have been.

are there any plot holes in the story?

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

[ASSISTANT] 2023-11-20T18:04:52.544954Z
Here are some potential plot holes I noticed in the screenplay:

- It's unclear why going back again and just watching the past fixes the timeline changes. Simply observing their past selves shouldn't undo the alterations they made before.

- It's inconsistent that Don and Jerry's memories seem unaffected after the timeline changes back. Logically, they should only remember the original timeline.

- The rules of time travel are vague. Like it's not clear why they turn into their past selves rather than just appearing as observers. Or why changing events takes time to catch up to the present.

- Voltec appears to have no oversight or approval process. Don and Jerry access time travel dangerously easily.

- The consequences for changing the past don't seem severe enough. The fix is simply going back again. There's no lasting punishment.

- Don and Jerry face little resistance or questioning when announcing Jerry as partner. The employees don't react much.

- The motives of Voltec's agents are unclear. They come across as plot devices rather than fully realized characters.

- It's not explained why going back and cheating is such a definitive failure point for Don's life. One incident wouldn't railroad his whole life.

So in summary, the mechanics of time travel in the story are a bit hand-wavy and create logical gaps. The consequences of altering the past also feel quite contained. Tighter rules and greater realism regarding the effects could plug some of those plot holes. But overall it's a fun escapist premise.

1) Introduction
2) Inciting incident/complication (changing the past)

4) Resolutions (acceptance)
5) Denouement (looking forward)
