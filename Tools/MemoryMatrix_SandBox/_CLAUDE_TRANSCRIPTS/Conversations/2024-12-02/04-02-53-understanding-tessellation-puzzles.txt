 Yes, I'd be happy to explain tessellation puzzles! A tessellation puzzle is a type of geometric puzzle where you need to completely fill a space with shapes (called tiles) without any gaps or overlaps. The word "tessellation" comes from the Latin "tessella," which refers to the small square stones used in ancient mosaics.

These puzzles can range from simple arrangements using regular shapes like squares or triangles to complex designs using irregular shapes that interlock in fascinating ways. A classic example is floor tiling, where identical squares need to fit together perfectly. More challenging versions might use pieces that can be rotated or flipped, or irregular shapes that only fit together in specific ways.

One famous example is the "pentomino puzzle," where you use all twelve possible shapes made from five connected squares to fill a rectangular area. Another well-known type is the "Wang tiles" puzzle, which uses squares with colored edges that must match their neighbors, similar to dominoes but with more complex matching rules.
