# Sovereign Knowledge Framework — 10 Layers
**Author:** Jero Wiku Hai Dai | Continental Council of Indigenous Elders
**Context:** Governance framework for AI systems serving indigenous cultural preservation

---

## 1. SOVEREIGNTY & GOVERNANCE (CORE LAYER)
**If this fails, everything fails.**

Key Question: Who holds power over knowledge, decisions, and access?

Critical Questions:
- Who owns the data? (individual, family, clan, council?)
- Who decides what enters the system?
- What governance body authorizes use?
- What are the rules for external sharing?
- Can permissions be revoked? How fast?
- What happens if governance structures change?
- How are disputes resolved?
- What legal + cultural frameworks are recognized?

Missing Gap to Design: A living governance charter + decision protocol system

## 2. DATA SOVEREIGNTY & INFRASTRUCTURE
**Where does the knowledge live?**

Key Question: Where is the knowledge stored and who physically controls it?

Critical Questions:
- Is all data local, decentralized, or hybrid?
- What happens if internet access is lost?
- Who has physical access to the storage?
- How is data encrypted and backed up?
- Can communities run their own instance independently?
- What happens if the system is shut down?
- Can data be migrated without loss or control?
- How is long-term preservation handled (generational)?

Missing Gap: Disaster recovery + generational continuity plan

## 3. KNOWLEDGE CLASSIFICATION & PROTECTION
**Not all knowledge is equal.**

Key Question: How do we differentiate sacred, restricted, and shareable knowledge?

Critical Questions:
- What categories of knowledge exist? (sacred, ceremonial, public, seasonal)
- Who defines these categories?
- How are TK labels assigned and updated?
- Can knowledge change classification over time?
- What knowledge should NEVER be digitized?
- How do we handle partial knowledge or fragmented stories?
- How do we prevent decontextualization?

Missing Gap: A culturally-specific ontology (not a generic taxonomy)

## 4. HUMAN ROLES & RELATIONAL STRUCTURE
**Technology doesn't replace relationships.**

Key Question: Who are the humans required for this system to function properly?

Critical Questions:
- Who are the intermediaries and how are they chosen?
- What training do intermediaries need?
- Who documents, translates, and validates knowledge?
- What is the role of youth vs elders?
- How is trust established between all roles?
- What happens if an intermediary misrepresents knowledge?
- How are relationships maintained over time?

Missing Gap: Defined roles + training pathways + accountability structures

## 5. KNOWLEDGE TRANSMISSION PROCESS
**How knowledge actually enters the system.**

Key Question: What is the right way for knowledge to be shared, witnessed, and recorded?

Critical Questions:
- What formats are allowed? (oral, ceremony, video, audio)
- When is recording appropriate vs not?
- How is consent captured in real-time?
- How do we preserve tone, context, emotion?
- What language(s) are prioritized?
- How is meaning validated with the elder?
- What happens after the session (review process)?

Missing Gap: Ceremonial + technical protocols for transmission

## 6. AI BEHAVIOR & ETHICS LAYER
**How the AI actually behaves.**

Key Question: What is the AI allowed and NOT allowed to do?

Critical Questions:
- What knowledge can the AI surface vs withhold?
- How does it communicate "restricted knowledge"?
- Can it summarize sacred knowledge—or only reference it?
- How does it cite sources and authority?
- What happens if a user asks something inappropriate?
- How does the AI handle conflicting knowledge?
- Can the AI learn autonomously? (should it?)

Missing Gap: Explicit "AI ethics constitution" tied to governance rules

## 7. LIFECYCLE, REVIEW & RENEWAL
**Knowledge is not static.**

Key Question: How does knowledge evolve, expire, or get corrected?

Critical Questions:
- How often is knowledge reviewed?
- Who initiates review cycles?
- What happens if knowledge is outdated or harmful?
- Can elders retract knowledge?
- How are corrections tracked?
- How do seasonal or ceremonial timelines affect access?
- What happens after an elder passes?

Missing Gap: Time-based governance + ancestral continuity protocols

## 8. COMMUNITY VALUE & BENEFIT (OUTER LAYER)
**Why this exists at all.**

Key Question: How does this system benefit the community—not just preserve data?

Critical Questions:
- How does this support youth learning?
- Does it strengthen or weaken cultural transmission?
- Who benefits economically (if at all)?
- How does this support land, water, and ecological systems?
- Does this reduce dependency on external systems?
- How does it build resilience?

Missing Gap: Clear value exchange + regenerative outcomes

## 9. RISK, HARM & FAILURE MODES
**What could go wrong? (this is critical and often skipped)**

Key Question: How could this system harm the community—and how do we prevent that?

Critical Questions:
- What happens if knowledge is leaked?
- What if governance is bypassed?
- What if AI misinterprets something sacred?
- What if external actors try to access the system?
- What if internal conflicts arise over knowledge ownership?
- What if the system creates dependency instead of empowerment?

Missing Gap: Threat modeling + cultural harm prevention protocols

## 10. ONBOARDING & TRUST BUILDING
**This is the BIGGEST gap identified.**

Key Question: How do elders actually begin?

Critical Questions:
- How do you explain this without technical language?
- What does the first interaction feel like?
- What reassurances are needed upfront?
- What are the boundaries clearly stated?
- How is consent experienced—not just signed?
- Who walks alongside the elder in the first session?

Missing Gap: A culturally aligned onboarding journey (not a document, an experience)
