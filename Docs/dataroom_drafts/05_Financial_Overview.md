# Financial Overview

## Year 1 Estimate

$70,000 -- $125,000 total. This covers core engine development, the Kinoni ICT Hub physical build-out, first-cohort programming, and operational costs.

## Budget Categories

- **Engineering:** Core engine development, Guardian community layer, Luganda language integration, on-device inference optimization.
- **Hardware & Infrastructure:** Kinoni ICT Hub build-out (solar power, devices, networking equipment, physical space).
- **Programming:** First youth cohort (12 participants, 6 months). Digital skills + cultural preservation curriculum.
- **Operations:** Travel, community coordination, partner communications, legal.
- **Contingency:** Buffer for the funding gaps we've learned to anticipate.

Detailed breakdowns are in Luna_Eclissi_Cost_Breakdown.xlsx and ca_tribal_cost_model.xlsx in this folder.

## Funding Sources Being Pursued

Active grant applications and partner conversations across indigenous-focused foundations, climate resilience funds, and technology-for-good programs. The EQC/ROSA partnership and Rotary relationship provide institutional credibility for grant applications.

## Revenue Model: The Forge

Luna is not a subscription product. The engine is open-source and sovereign by design -- charging for access would undermine the whole point.

Revenue comes through The Forge: implementation services. Communities, organizations, and institutions that want Luna deployed for their knowledge management needs hire us to configure, customize, and support the deployment. We build the tools. Communities own the result.

This means revenue scales with adoption, not extraction. Every deployment makes the next one easier. The communities we serve never become dependent on us.
