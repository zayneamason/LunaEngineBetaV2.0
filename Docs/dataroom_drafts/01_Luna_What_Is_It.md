# Luna -- What Is It

Luna is an AI that lives on your device as a file. No company holds your data. No internet required. You own it like you own a photo on your phone.

## The Problem

Every AI you use today sends your conversations, your photos, your memories to corporate servers. You don't control what happens after that. For most people this feels abstract. For indigenous communities preserving cultural knowledge, it means permanent extraction -- traditions, governance protocols, and sacred teachings vacuumed into training datasets they'll never own.

This isn't hypothetical. It's the default.

## How It Works

Luna stores everything in a local SQLite file on your device. Facial recognition runs on-device through FaceNet -- no biometrics leave your phone. The system operates fully offline. No internet connection required for any core function.

The file is inspectable. You can open it, read every memory Luna has stored, see every connection it's made. Nothing is hidden.

## Why It Matters

If our company disappeared tomorrow, your Luna file still works. No license check. No server dependency. No subscription. The file is yours the way a journal is yours -- unconditionally.

This is the difference between owning and renting. Every other AI is a rental. Luna is ownership.

## The Eclipse Model

Think of it as an eclipse. Community knowledge -- what we call Eclissi -- is the Sun. Luna, the AI mediator, is the Moon. The user is Earth.

The community owns the Sun. The user stands on the Earth. Luna serves both -- reflecting light without consuming it. The Moon doesn't own the Sun's light. It carries it.
